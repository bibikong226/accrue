## Design Requirements

These requirements derive from UX and behavioral finance literature. Every requirement has a rationale. Do not simplify away any requirement without flagging it — each one is academically justified and thesis-defensible.

---

### 1. Onboarding & Trust-Building

**Conversational multi-step registration, never a dense form.**
Collect risk tolerance, financial goals, experience level, and time horizon across short, focused steps. One question or decision per screen. At the end of onboarding the AI copilot introduces itself and explains what it will do. This should feel like a one-to-one conversation, not a form to get through.

**Progress bar on every registration step.**
Every step must display its position: "Step 2 of 5: Your Goals." The progress bar is not optional decoration — it is a direct implementation of Nielsen's visibility of system status heuristic and has a documented effect on reducing user anxiety during onboarding.

**Security reassurance at the point of registration.**
Data handling and privacy information must appear during registration, not buried in terms of service. A one-line plain-language statement on the relevant step is sufficient: "Your information is encrypted and never sold."

**Progressive disclosure on the dashboard.**
The dashboard default state shows only: total portfolio value, headline performance, primary action (e.g., "Make a trade"). Asset-level breakdowns, advanced analytics, and secondary settings are behind expandable sections or deeper navigation. They do not appear until the user actively navigates toward them.

---

### 2. AI Copilot

**Copilot is a collapsible panel or activatable icon present on every page.** It is never the only path to information — it supplements content that is already accessible as readable HTML.

**What the copilot is allowed to say:**
- Explain what the user is currently looking at in plain language
- Summarize trends using data injected from the app's data layer
- Flag concentration risk or diversification issues before a trade
- Answer questions about financial terms using the predefined glossary (see Glossary section below)
- Surface goal-alignment concerns: "If you buy this, tech stocks will make up 60% of your portfolio."

**What the copilot must never do:**
- Generate, estimate, round, or infer any financial number not present in the injected data context
- Present a recommendation without a confidence signal
- Use financial jargon without defining it
- Speak with certainty about anything it cannot verify

**AI-generated text annotation.**
Every response surface produced by the AI copilot must carry a visible label: a small "AI" badge or "Generated by AI" attribution inline with the response. This is not a disclaimer — it is a permanent UI element on every AI output so users always know what is interpretation versus verified data.

**Confidence signals on every AI output.**
AI-generated outputs must carry one of three confidence levels: `Low`, `Moderate`, or `High`. Implement as a small indicator attached to the response card. For consequential outputs — risk assessments, goal-alignment checks, trade recommendations — a `Low` confidence signal must trigger a visible prompt: "Verify this in the Research section before acting."

**Source attribution on factual claims.**
Every AI claim that draws on financial data must display a source label and a last-updated timestamp: "Source: Portfolio data · Updated just now." This distinguishes verified information from AI interpretation.

**Copilot context is injected, never generated.**
See the Hallucination Prevention section in this file. The copilot data context is always built from `/src/data/mockPortfolio.ts`. The AI narrates; the app supplies the numbers.

### 3. Glossary — Predefined Terms, Never AI-Generated

**The glossary is a static hardcoded module, not an AI output.**

Every financial term that appears anywhere on the platform must have a tooltip. The tooltip text comes from `/src/data/glossary.ts`, not from the AI. The AI may reference glossary terms in its responses but must use the exact predefined definition text, passed in via the system prompt, not generated inline.

```ts
// src/data/glossary.ts
// All definitions are predefined. The AI does not write these.
export const glossary: Record<string, string> = {
  "P/E ratio": "The price-to-earnings ratio compares a company's stock price to its earnings per share. A higher P/E means investors expect more future growth.",
  "market cap": "Market capitalization is the total value of a company's shares. A higher market cap usually means a larger, more established company.",
  "yield": "Yield is the income an investment generates, expressed as a percentage of its price. A bond yielding 4% pays $4 per year for every $100 invested.",
  "diversification": "Diversification means spreading your money across different investments to reduce risk. If one investment loses value, others may not.",
  "concentration risk": "Concentration risk is the danger of having too much of your money in one investment or sector. If that investment drops, your whole portfolio is affected.",
  "limit order": "A limit order executes only if the price reaches a level you set. It gives you control over price but no guarantee the order will fill.",
  "market order": "A market order executes immediately at the current price. It fills quickly but the exact price may differ slightly from what you saw.",
  "stop order": "A stop order sells automatically if the price falls to a level you set. It is used to limit losses on a position.",
  "analyst rating": "An analyst rating is a recommendation from a financial professional: Strong Buy, Buy, Hold, Sell, or Strong Sell.",
  // ... extend as needed
};
```

**Tooltip implementation.**
Every instance of a glossary term in the UI must be wrapped in a `<GlossaryTerm>` component that renders the predefined definition on hover and focus. The term must be visually distinguishable (underline or dotted border) so users know it is interactive.

```tsx
// src/components/GlossaryTerm.tsx
// a11y: role="tooltip" + aria-describedby connects the term to its definition for screen readers
<span
  tabIndex={0}
  aria-describedby={tooltipId}
  className="underline decoration-dotted cursor-help"
>
  {term}
</span>
<div id={tooltipId} role="tooltip">
  {glossary[term]}
</div>
```

### 4. Portfolio Dashboard

**Single consolidated overview, no scrolling required for primary metrics.**
Total portfolio value, allocation breakdown, and performance summary must be visible above the fold. Use a `<dl>` description list for the headline metrics so VoiceOver reads label and value together.

**Customizable dashboard layout.**
Users can add, remove, or reorder dashboard widgets: watchlist, AI insights panel, goal progress tracker, news feed. Widget state persists to localStorage. Reordering must be keyboard accessible — use arrow keys to move a focused widget.

**Asset allocation visualization with diversification indicator.**
Show portfolio composition as a donut chart (with an accessible data table and text summary — see Chart rules). Alongside the chart, display:
- A plain-language diversification gauge: `Low` / `Moderate` / `Well Diversified`
- A one-sentence explanation specific to the user's portfolio: "70% of your portfolio is in one stock. Spreading across more investments could reduce your risk."

**Performance figures must never appear in isolation.**
Every performance number must be framed relative to:
1. The user's stated goal (from onboarding)
2. A relevant benchmark

Example: "Your portfolio is up 3.56% since you started. Your goal is 8% annual growth. You are on track." Never display a raw percentage with no context.

**Forward-looking metrics alongside historical data.**
Display analyst forecasts, price targets, and earnings estimates alongside past performance. Past performance alone is a documented design failure mode that encourages anchoring to historical price trends.

**Goal progress tracker.**
Display a progress indicator: distance to stated goal, projected pace, estimated time to goal at current trajectory. Frame in data, not celebratory animation.

### 5. Order Entry & Trade Flow

**Mandatory review screen — no exceptions.**
No trade may execute without passing through a review screen. The review screen must display:
- Asset symbol and full name
- Action (Buy / Sell)
- Quantity (shares)
- Order type with plain-language description
- Price (estimated)
- All fees, itemized
- Projected portfolio impact after this trade
- AI copilot plain-language consequence: "After this trade, tech stocks will be 60% of your portfolio."

The user must take an explicit confirm action from this screen. The first submit click never executes a trade.

**Upfront fee disclosure.**
All costs must appear in a structured breakdown before the review screen. No costs may appear for the first time after confirmation.

**AI risk flag before confirmation.**
The copilot runs a pre-trade check and surfaces any goal-alignment or diversification concern on the review screen. The flag is prominent but not blocking — the user can still proceed after reading it.

### 6. Research & Financial Education

**Two layers of education — passive inline and deliberate research.**

*Passive inline layer:*
- Glossary tooltips on every financial term, everywhere on the platform
- One-line context cards when viewing a stock: "AAPL is in the Technology sector. You currently hold 10 shares."
- AI copilot available on demand on every page without navigating away

*Deliberate research section:*
- A distinct navigable section that surfaces: company fundamentals, analyst ratings, earnings history, price targets, sector context, and news filtered to a specific asset
- The AI copilot inside the research section acts as a guide: it summarizes what the data means and flags anything that conflicts with the user's stated goals or risk profile

**Education does not interrupt active trading flows.**
The inline layer is passive — tooltips appear on demand, not automatically. The research section is a deliberate destination. These are two separate layers. Do not merge them or redirect a user mid-trade to a research page.

### 7. Motivation & Engagement

**Goal-setting and milestone tracking framed in data, not celebration.**
Allow users to define a financial goal and track progress against it. Progress indicators use data: amounts, percentages, projected dates. No confetti, no reward animations. Hedonic gamification elements reduce users' perception of investment risk and are explicitly excluded from this platform.

**News feed tied to holdings only.**
Surface news about assets the user actually owns. No trending stocks, no popularity rankings, no volume-based sorting. The AI copilot summarizes each article and connects it to the user's portfolio: "This news affects your AAPL holding. Here is what it means for your position."

**No features that conflate investing with gambling.**
Do not implement: leaderboards, streak counters, badges for trade frequency, reward sounds, or any engagement pattern that encourages trading for its own sake.

### 8. AI Accuracy, Bias, and Hallucination Prevention (Design Layer)

This section extends the technical hallucination prevention rules (already defined above) with the design-level patterns that make AI reliability visible to users.

**Three-part trust model for AI outputs:**
1. **Attribution** — where did this data come from and when was it last updated
2. **Confidence signal** — `Low` / `Moderate` / `High` attached to every AI output
3. **Uncertainty language** — when the copilot is interpreting rather than reporting, the language must reflect that: "Based on your current holdings, it looks like..." not "Your portfolio will..."

**Low-confidence outputs must prompt verification.**
When a confidence signal is `Low`, the copilot response card shows: "This is an interpretation, not verified data. Check the Research section or an external source before acting on this."

**The copilot does not replace the research section.**
It summarizes and explains. For any output where acting on incorrect information could cause financial harm, the copilot must link to the underlying source data the user can verify independently.

### 9. Mock Data Completeness Checklist

Before building any UI component, confirm `/src/data/mockPortfolio.ts` contains all values that component will display. Add fields to the mock data module first, then build the component. No component may render a financial figure that does not exist in the mock data module at build time.

Required fields by section:
- **Dashboard:** `totalValue`, `cashBalance`, `totalGainLoss`, `totalGainLossPercent`, `holdings[]`, `allocationBySector{}`, `diversificationRating`, `goalProgress{}`
- **Holdings table:** per holding — `symbol`, `name`, `shares`, `avgCost`, `currentPrice`, `marketValue`, `gainLoss`, `gainLossPercent`, `direction`
- **Charts:** per holding — `priceHistory{ "1D"|"1W"|"1M"|"3M"|"1Y": DataPoint[] }`
- **Order entry:** `estimatedFees`, `currentAskPrice`, `projectedPortfolioImpact{}`
- **Research:** per company — `fundamentals{}`, `analystRatings{}`, `earningsHistory[]`, `priceTarget`, `relatedNews[]`
- **Transactions:** `transactions[]` with `date`, `symbol`, `tradeType`, `quantity`, `price`, `total`
- **Copilot context:** built dynamically from the above at call time via `buildCopilotPrompt()`
